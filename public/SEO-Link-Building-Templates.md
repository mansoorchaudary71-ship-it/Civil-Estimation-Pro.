### Example Helpful Answer Template (Reddit / Eng-Tips)
**Topic:** Someone asking how to calculate steel for a footing.
**Reply:** 
Great question. For standard isolated footings, you need to calculate the cutting length of the bottom mesh. 

First, deduct the clear cover (usually 50mm if resting on PCC) from both ends of your footing dimension. Then, add the vertical L-bend hooks (usually 150mm to 200mm upwards) for anchorage. Multiply the total length by the number of bars. 

If you want to skip the manual math, I actually built a completely free **[Isolated Footing Calculator]** that generates the exact steel weight and concrete volume automatically. It accounts for trapezoidal frustum volumes too. Hope this helps your project!
